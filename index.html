Overview

Model: 2021 Surveyor Legend 320BHLE Travel Trailer (37 feet)

Key Features:

  • Length: 37 feet
  • Dry Weight: 7,300 lbs
  • Sleeping Capacity: Up to 10 people
  • Outdoor Kitchen: Mini-fridge, grill, and sink
  • Spacious living and dining area with U-shaped dinette
  • Full bathroom with shower, toilet, and sink

Pros & Cons

Pros:

  • Large, family-friendly layout with a bunkhouse and private master bedroom.
  • Outdoor kitchen for easy meal prep during camping.
  • High-quality interior finishes, including hardwood cabinetry and durable countertops.
  • Spacious living area with a U-shaped dinette and sleeper sofa.

Cons:

  • Heavy weight requires at least a ¾-ton truck for towing.
  • Some reported issues with minor leaks around the slide-outs.
  • Limited kitchen counter space inside the RV.
  • Higher maintenance requirements due to the size and complexity of the slide-out mechanisms.

Checklist for Buying a Used RV

  • Check for water damage (especially around slide-outs, windows, and roof).
  • Inspect seals and gaskets for wear or cracks.
  • Test all appliances (A/C, refrigerator, microwave, etc.) to ensure they work properly.
  • Inspect tires for wear and check the manufacturing date (tires older than 5 years should be replaced).
  • Ask for maintenance records and verify regular servicing.
  • Check the RV’s title for any liens or accident history.
  • Test slide-outs for smooth operation and check the condition of the rubber seals.
  • Inspect the undercarriage for rust or structural damage.
  • Ensure the RV’s braking system is functional.
  • Verify the condition of the batteries and electrical systems.

Questions to Ask the Seller

  • How long have you owned the RV?
  • Why are you selling it?
  • Do you have the maintenance records?
  • Have there been any accidents or repairs made?
  • Are all the appliances and systems functioning properly?
  • Has the RV been stored indoors or outdoors?
  • Have there been any warranty claims, and are any warranties still transferable?
  • How many miles has the RV been towed?
  • Have you made any modifications or upgrades?

Negotiation Tips for Buying a Used RV

  • **Research Market Value**: Use websites like NADA Guides to check the current market value of the RV.
  • **Point Out Issues**: Highlight any issues you find during your inspection (e.g., wear and tear, water damage, etc.) to negotiate a lower price.
  • **Be Prepared to Walk Away**: Don’t hesitate to walk away if the seller won’t budge on price. There are always other RVs available.
  • **Offer Cash**: If you can, offering cash upfront can often help you negotiate a better deal.
  • **Ask for Extras**: Try to get the seller to include extras like towing accessories, RV cover, or any other items they may no longer need.
